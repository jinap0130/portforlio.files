CHYPRE

With facets of citrus fruits, flower and wood, chypre perfumes single-handedly combine multiple different olfactory families in perfumery. Bergamot and rose are frequently included in the composition.

EAU ROSE

Eau de parfum

Damascena and Centifolia roses, Chamomile, Litchi, Artichoke


Capricious and exuberant. In Eau Rose eau de parfum, this emblematic flower of perfumers shows off its more flamboyant aspects. Within the bottle, absolute and extract of damascena and centifolia roses, contribute their full intensity. Essence of the firad rose, with its unexpected accents of chamomile and artichoke, rounds off the bouquet.

EAU CAPITALE

Eau de parfum

Rose, Bergamot, Patchouli, Pink peppercorn


Like a tribute to Paris. For Diptyque, only a chypre - the accord with a thousand facets - could portray this city with a thousand faces. In Eau Capitale eau de parfum, bergamot - given extra bite by pink peppercorn - is followed by roses, and moss gives way to patchouli. Paris in a bottle.

WOODY

Woody perfumes have the strength and density of the woods they symbolize, made smoky and elegant by vetiver or creamy and spicy by sandalwood, or evoking the deep, earthy nuances of patchouli.

VETYVERIO

Eau de parfum

etiver from Java, Vetiver from Haiti, Grapefruit, Rose


Beyond feminine, beyond masculine. Vetyverio eau de parfum reveals facets that are sometimes smoky, sometimes elegant, sometimes subtly floral. Used in an overdose, vetiver transcends gender. A kaleidoscope of nuances within a single raw material, magnified here in an unusual interpretation. Like inlaid wood.

PHILOSYKOSEAU CAPITALE

Eau de parfum

Fig leaves, Fig tree sap, Fig tree wood, Black pepper


Fresh perspectives on the fig tree. In Philosykos eau de parfum, the flavour of the fruit cedes centre stage, and white cedar underscores the woody strength of the tree and its bark. A fragrance that carries the scent of a memory: crossing a grove of wild fig trees during a summer in Greece.
